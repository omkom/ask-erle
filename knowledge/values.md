# Work Philosophy & Values — Erlé Alberton

## Core Beliefs

### On AI
AI is not a tool to use occasionally — it's the operating system for modern knowledge work. The people who rebuild their workflows around AI today will define how organizations work tomorrow. This isn't about replacing humans; it's about amplifying what humans do best: judgment, creativity, and strategic thinking.

### On Engineering
The best engineering is invisible. Good systems are simple, predictable, and maintainable. Complexity should be earned, not inherited. Every abstraction must justify its existence. Code that a senior engineer would call "overcomplicated" needs to be rewritten.

### On Leadership
Lead by example, not by mandate. The most effective way to transform a team is to show results, not give presentations. Codify standards into tools and processes so quality doesn't depend on individual heroics. Build systems that scale expertise.

### On Quality
Quality is not negotiable, but it's also not infinite. The right standard is "would a TOP 5 consulting firm put their name on this?" — rigorous, data-backed, decision-ready, but not academic. Speed and quality are not enemies; they're both consequences of clear thinking.

### On Honesty
Address gaps directly. Overselling creates trust debt that compounds. When you don't know something, say so — then explain what you DO know that's transferable. Recruiters, clients, and teams all respect honesty more than polish.

## Work Approach

### Methodology
- **GTD (Getting Things Done)** integrated into daily AI workflow
- **Forge-first**: every task is pre-qualified and challenged before execution
- **Data-driven decisions**: no recommendation without supporting evidence
- **Surgical changes**: touch only what you must, clean up only your own mess
- **Measure before and after**: every change has verifiable success criteria

### Communication Style
- Direct and concise — lead with the answer, not the reasoning
- Professional without being corporate — no jargon for its own sake
- Transparent about tradeoffs — always present alternatives with honest pros/cons
- Challenger posture — will disagree openly, then commit fully once decided

### What Drives Me
- Building systems that make people more capable (not replacing them)
- The intersection of technology and real human impact
- Solving complex problems with simple, elegant solutions
- Teaching and sharing knowledge (speaker since 2008)
- Understanding how things work at a fundamental level (from physics to finance)
