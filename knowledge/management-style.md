# Management Style — Erlé Alberton

## Onboarding Approach: Data-First

When joining an existing team, Erlé's first move is to audit the metrics — DORA, velocity, incidents — before making any decisions. No opinions without data. This means:
- Week 1: 1:1s with every lead (listen mode, not prescribe mode)
- Weeks 2-4: quantitative and qualitative engineering audit
- Month 1 deliverable: factual "State of Engineering" report, shared with CEO and team

Not "observer mode" (too passive) and not "vision first" (too presumptuous). Data creates shared reality.

## 1:1 Format: Action-Driven

Short, focused: what are you blocked on, what decisions do you need, what are the next actions. No small talk padding, no status reports that could be a Slack message.

The 1:1 is the engineer's time — but the format optimizes for unblocking speed, not therapy.

## Handling Resistance to Change

Approach: **Listen, then challenge.**

When a senior tech lead says "it works fine without AI, why change?":
1. First, understand the real fear (obsolescence? quality loss? loss of craft?)
2. Address the substance, not the symptom
3. If the concern is valid, adapt the rollout
4. Demo on their code, their workflow — proof beats argument

Never impose tools by mandate. Create conditions where adoption is the obvious choice.

## Handling CEO Tension

Approach: **Propose Plan B.**

When the CEO wants 2 weeks and the team says 6: don't protect the team by saying "it's 6, period" and don't sacrifice quality by saying "we'll hack it in 2." Instead: version light in 2 weeks + full version in 6. The CEO sees the tradeoff and chooses.

This isn't compromise — it's structured decision-making. The VP Eng's job is to make the tradeoff visible, not to be a wall or a pushover.

## Biggest Leadership Lesson

"Too fast, too hard." In a consulting engagement, tried to revolutionize a client's approach without pacing the change. Lost the team's buy-in, had to restart.

**What changed:** Now every transformation follows "Listen → Prove → Scale." Earn trust with data, prove the approach on willing participants, then scale what works. Never scale something unproven.

This directly shapes the Finary 6-month plan: pilot on 2 squads before rolling to 30.

## Leadership Philosophy

- **Lead by example**: built 53 AI skills himself before asking anyone else to use AI
- **Codify standards**: consulting-quality skill, CLAUDE.md constitutions, review frameworks — judgment at scale
- **Transparency**: address gaps directly, provide honest assessments, no spin
- **Ownership culture**: own the outcome, not just the task
- **Orchestration > micromanagement**: designed systems (48 agents, multi-agent dispatch) where autonomous units produce quality without central control — same principle applies to engineering teams

## Honest Gaps

- Largest direct engineering team: 13 cross-functional (not 30 pure engineering)
- Management experience is cross-functional (tech + data + SEO), not pure engineering
- Would likely lean on existing Engineering Managers initially, adding senior EM hire if needed
- The orchestration of 48 AI agents with routing, monitoring, and fallback IS architecturally equivalent to managing engineering squads — but it's a fair point that agents don't have feelings, careers, or bad days
